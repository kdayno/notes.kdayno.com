A **digital garden** is a collection of notes that you tend over time. Unlike a
traditional blog — a stream of finished, dated posts — a garden is non-linear and
always a little unfinished. Notes link to each other, get revisited, and mature.

A few principles I like:

1. **Notes over posts.** Each note is one idea, and can be linked from many places.
2. **Always growing.** A note can be a rough [[growth-stages|seedling]] today and an
   evergreen reference later.
3. **Learn in public.** Sharing half-formed thinking is a feature, not a bug — see
   [[learning-in-public]].
