I used to wait until an idea felt "finished" before writing it down anywhere others
could see. The problem: ideas are rarely finished, so I rarely shared anything.

A garden flips that. A note can start as a rough [[growth-stages|seedling]] — a single
paragraph, maybe even wrong — and still be useful. Writing it down forces me to make
the idea concrete, and linking it to other notes ([[what-is-a-digital-garden]]) shows me
where my thinking has gaps.

This note lives in a different folder (`content/thoughts/`) than the others
(`content/notes/`). Notice that Quartz automatically builds a **folder page** for each
folder and a path you can browse in the **Explorer** on the left.

> [!quote]
> "You should blog with the assumption that no one will read it, and write the post that
> you wish you had found." — a paraphrase I keep coming back to.
